How it works

Two loops. One dataset.

Most teams either test their AI in CI or watch it in production. We do both — against the same dataset — so a regression that ships is the same diff you catch in prod.

1. Regression suite

Catch breakage before deploy

1
Capture a known-good dataset

Import the input/output pairs your downstream rules depend on — straight from production, an export, or hand-crafted edge cases.

2
Replay on every change

Whenever you swap models, edit prompts, or change code, run the dataset through your endpoint. Field-level structured diffs flag exactly which JSON fields drifted — with per-field tolerance bands.

3
Approve or fail the deploy

Reviewers approve expected changes; unexpected drift fails the run. CI gates on the result. Auditors get the trail.

2. Live audit

Monitor what actually happens

1
Sample production traffic

One SDK call per AI decision. Sample by rate, by confidence, by metadata. Buffered, async, zero added latency.

2
Score against the same rules

The same field-level diff and tolerance bands you use in CI run against live traffic. When prod drifts from your dataset, you find out before your customers do.

3
Promote findings into the suite

One click turns a flagged production case into a regression test — closing the loop so the next deploy can't repeat the failure.

Self-hosted in your own infra

Open-source MIT-style license. docker compose up on a VM, your VPC, an air-gapped network, your laptop — your data, your model traffic, your auditors. No regres.ai cloud required.

Field-level structured diff

Built for JSON outputs that gate deterministic downstream systems. Per-field tolerance, severity classification, and exact-match enforcement on the fields where exact actually matters.

Audit-ready trail

Every change, every reviewer decision, every run logged with timestamp + actor. Exportable for compliance review. RBAC for reviewer-only seats. Plus per-org LLM choice (Anthropic, AWS Bedrock) with credentials encrypted at rest.

Where this fits

If your AI's output gates a deterministic downstream system, you need this.

Not for chatbots. Not for content generation. For workflows where a wrong field on a JSON response causes a wrong claim, a wrong payout, a wrong denial, or an audit finding.

Pharmacy & prior authorization

Adjudication, formulary checks, refills. When approval or denial fields drift, downstream rules misroute the script.

Insurance claims & underwriting

Coverage decisions, fraud flags, payouts. Field-level diff on policy IDs and loss codes — not a confidence score.

KYC, AML & credit decisioning

Identity, risk, sanctions screening. Prove a model swap didn't quietly change who gets approved.

Mortgage origination & servicing

Doc extraction, qualification, exceptions. Tolerance for amounts and dates; exact match for borrower IDs.

Built by the team that automated production pharmacy claim processing — where a wrong AI field meant the wrong patient outcome, and where the output had to be exact enough for downstream rule engines to act on it.

Honest comparison

Why not Langfuse, Braintrust, or LangSmith?

They're great tools for ML engineers building chatbots and content. We're not trying to win that. We're built for one thing: open-source regression testing for AI workflows where the output is structured JSON that downstream rules act on, in industries where compliance asks "prove it."

Field-level structured diff, not free-text scoring

Their evaluators are built for narrative outputs scored by an LLM judge. We compare JSON paths exactly, with per-field tolerance bands. When decision changes from "approved" to "denied", you find out — not because a judge LLM gave a 7.4 instead of an 8.1.

Self-host the whole stack, not just the SDK

Closed-source competitors ship hosted-only or single-tenant-as-a-paid-add-on. Langfuse is open source but horizontal. We're both — open source, self-hosted in any environment, and shaped specifically for regulated decision systems out of the box.

Regression and live audit, on the same dataset

Most competitors do one. The few that do both don't connect them. We do — a flagged production case becomes a regression test in one click, so the same drift can't ship twice.

Built for compliance buyers, not just ML engineers

RBAC, audit trail, exportable reports, dashboards your risk officer can use without provisioning Postgres themselves. Per-org LLM choice (Anthropic, AWS Bedrock) keeps your model traffic where your compliance posture requires.
